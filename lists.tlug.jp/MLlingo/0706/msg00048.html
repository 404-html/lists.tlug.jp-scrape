I've met J who can and mostly do speak their language in a perfectly
straightforward and unambiguous way, and I've met others who habitually
indulge themselves in the opposite direction to the point where even my
(native-speaking) wife - herself definitely on the more-ambiguous end of

Mauro has noticed that a lot of Japanese instruction manuals are hard
to follow for Japanese people due to the lack of contextual clues
typically garnered from body language, &c.

I think the theory to be extrapolated from this is that Japanese are
not good at reading things that belong to groups of things that they
do not often read.
