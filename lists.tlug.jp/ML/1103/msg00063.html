Hi Jong,

> i want to clean my processor fan  but can't do it without the compressed air
> can.
> where can i get compressed air can here in tokyo?
>
They are called "air duster" here (in Japanese EA--DA-SU-TA-A,
エアダスター). Any of the big electronic shops also carries them, e.g.
Yodobashi or BicCamera:
http://www.yodobashi.com/ec/category/index.html?word=%E3%82%A8%E3%82%A2%E3%83%80%E3%82%B9%E3%82%BF%E3%83%BC
http://www.biccamera.co.jp/shoplist/index_english.html


Be careful not to use them too much in a closed room, esp. close to a
LP gas leakage sensor (usually found on the ceiling in the kitchen). I
usually do that outside on the balcony. (it is not really air inside,
but different gases that trigger the sensor).

Although a bit more expensive, you are better off with the ones that
can be turned upside down (without spitting liquid), e.g.:
http://www.yodobashi.com/ec/product/100000001000956177/index.html

If you are really worried about health or sensors, get a CO2 one
(expensive, with refills):
http://www.yodobashi.com/ec/product/100000001000885717/index.html

> or is there any alternative to air cans in cleaning the processor/fan?
>
Use a vacuum cleaner with a thin attachment.
Get a long straw/narrow hose and blow (without spitting).

Cheers,
Kalin.
