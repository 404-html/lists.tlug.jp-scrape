Yes, but I think the law required the smoking and non-smoking ones to
be actually separated.  I recall seeing restaurants prior to April 1,
2010 have both seats and then just weeks before that date, they
renovated and added a door.  Of course, the smoke still passes through
when the door is opened...it isn't perfect -- but it did seem to force
them to try harder than to just designate seats as being smoking and
non-smoking.

After being too used to Kanagawa, I went to a Tokyo Watami [I think]
and asked for non-smoking seats...boy, did I get a strange look from
the staff!  :-)

Oh...you could have the nomikai at a Starbucks -- they're all non-smoking!  ;-)
