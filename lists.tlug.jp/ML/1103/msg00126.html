Hi JC,

That depends on what you actually want to do.
AFAIK, there is no replacement for iTunes that would do the 
things iTunes does (such as (mis)manage your i-devices, download & stream etc from the app store).

There are many music players available, though. 
I use mpg321, mostly to stream radio. But it's not for everyone.

> I tried to move my iTunes music library to external HDD and have now
> entered iTunes music Library hell. I've spent 3 evenings trying to
> recover and have now decided to cut my loses and give up.

I feel your pain.

iTunes splits things in two, the database and the actual media files.
You can have the media files anywhere, but the database is always in ~/Music/iTunes Library

I think copying iTunes/iPhoto stuff needs to be handled on the command line.
What has worked for me is to manually copy ~/Music/iTunes Media like

 rsync -a /Users/hs/Music/iTunes\ Media/ /Volumes/BIGEXTERNALDISK/backup/

Then move the library file away and reimport this whole folder in iTunes.
You can keep the media organized in one folder by checking that preference in iTunes.
