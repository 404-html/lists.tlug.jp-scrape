On Mon, Dec 11, 2000 at 01:14:10PM +0900, Hector Akamine wrote:

> For example, my video card supports 1024x768 32-bit, 1152x864 32-bit but
> doesn't have enough memory to support 1280x1024 with 32-bit colors.

Yargh.  32bpp is not a well-supported color depth.  In fact, 32bpp is
usually really 24bpp.

Why would you want to have such a high color depth?  The human eye
generally can't tell the difference between anything higher than 15bpp.
Further, high color depths adversely impact video card performance.

But, to answer your question ...

> Is  it possible for example to cycle to 1280x1024 16 or 8 bit in the same
> way (Ctrl+Alt+ +)?

No.
