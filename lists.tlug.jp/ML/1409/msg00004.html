You're comparing apples and oranges. wi2-300 is for using in cafes,
restaurants, hairdressers, etc. I suspect it is partly subsidized by the
shops.

And it is public wifi, with all the security risks that entails.
(wi2premium_club connections are encrypted, but I'm betting the routers
are sitting on an open shelf in the kitchen areas...)

Having said that, it *is* not just cheap but good. Fast enough and they
don't block ports (so SSH works, the secure SMTP/POP ports work, etc.)
