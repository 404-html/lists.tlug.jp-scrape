Neither, I suspect.  In sane packages, `make clean' deletes
config.cache.  RMS points out, quite correctly, that this makes
config.cache useless for most packages  So most GNU packages keep it
around, risking bogus behavior.

It is a _big_ timesaver if you're building, say, the whole Cygnus
toolchain.  It's just plain dangerous for most packages, though, and
results in confusing behavior.
