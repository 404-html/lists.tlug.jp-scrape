> Here am I to bug y'all with another question.  I want to
> buy an internal-type CDROM drive for my Linux-to-be machine.
> Do I have to worry a lot that Linux is not listed as one
> of the supported OSes on the box?  One brand I've seen

Virtually any ATAPI (IDE) CD-ROM that is 4X or faster will work fine
with Linux.  (It is not actually the speed that is the requirement,
its just that the ATAPI specification was open to a bit of "interpretation"
prior to about the time the 4X drives appeared.  Nearly all early drives
will also work, but some may display "quirks.")
