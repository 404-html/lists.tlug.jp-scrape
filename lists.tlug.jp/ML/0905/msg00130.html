And I suspect, if you do something like:

setxkbmap -rules xfree86 jp

your keyboard will magically start working again the way it once did.
If you like that and want to keep it, you can add a line to your
/etc/X11/xorg.conf's InputDevice section:

        Option      "XkbRules"   "xfree86"
