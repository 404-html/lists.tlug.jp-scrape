On 28/05/09 07:51, Stephen J. Turnbull wrote:

> Do check.  The Zaurus SLC-3000 driver has a silly limitation of 1GiB on
> SD cards.  I could understand 2GiB (32-bit ssize_t) or 4GiB (32 bit
> size_t), but 1GiB?[1]

This often happens when manufacturers don't look to the future.

It is probably that 1GB[0] was the largest size card available at the time
the SLC-3000 driver was developed, so that's the largest size card supported.


[0] - yes, GB not GiB, SD card manufacturers use the same tricks as hard
disk manufacturers, so a 1GB card will have roughly a billion bytes
capacity, not 2^30 (1,073,741,824) bytes.
