-=-=-=- Announcement: 2010-10-09 TLUG Technical Meeting -=-=-=-=-

Meeting information

   Date: October 9th (Saturday)

   Time: 14:00 - 16:30

   Place: Plat'Home Co., Ltd.
          Akihabara Daibiru 9th floor
          1-18-13 Sotokanda,
          Chiyoda-Ku, Tokyo, Japan

   Details:
http://www.tlug.jp/wiki/Meetings:Locations:Akihabara:Plat'home

   Topics)

          "Report on JavaOne in SFO"
          By Masafumi Ohta

          "Hudson continuous build/integration system"
          By Lars Kotthoff

          "Fun with rooted Android firmware"
          By Kyle Hasagawa

This month, we will have a different format with four short talks.

The first speaker Masafumi Ohta will give a report on JavaOne in SFO.
This year is the first time Sun-Oracle has held this event and it will
be interesting to hear what they have to say about the future of MySQL,
Oracle EE Linux, Oracle VM (Zen) virtualization.

The second speaker Lars Kotthoff will give a presentation on the Hudson
continuous build/integration system. The talk will cover some theory
followed by a demonstration.

The third speaker Kyle Hasagawa will talk about using alternative
firmware with Android phones. The talk will cover installation,
management, backup and recovery, as well as Apps2SD, Uninstalling
uninstallable apps, over-clocking and other features.

If you are involved in a project or have something you would like to
talk about in this or a future technical meeting, we are always looking
for presenters.

Other items on the agenda are:
1. Introduction of new members, news about Linux/Open Source,
general announcements, Q&A
2. Auction

All Linux users and supporters of open source code and free software
are invited to attend this Tokyo Linux Users Group (TLUG) meeting.
Membership is open to anyone. There are no membership dues or entrance
fees.

After the meeting, we will continue chatting over a few beers.

About TLUG
----------
TLUG is a non-profit, non-political organization established in June
of 1994 to exchange information on the use of Linux and Free Software
Foundation tools. TLUG has an active mailing list, home page, and
regular physical meetings. Discussions are primarily held in English,
but non-English speakers are encouraged to come and participate.
We especially want to encourage Japanese people to attend and will make
efforts to support a bilingual meeting.

TLUG generally hosts technical meetings every month on the 2nd
Saturday, and a nomikai (social even at a pub) every even month on the
3rd Friday.

The TLUG home page is available athttp://www.tlug.jp
