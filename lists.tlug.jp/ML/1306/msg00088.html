For historical reference the router in question is a WarpStar Aterm WR4500N
(which someone gave me and I was going to use to experiment with, but it
turned out to be better than my existing router).

According to "Wifi Analyzer" running on a Nexus 7, outside the room where it's
located, the signal is between  -50 and -60 Dbm throughout most of the house
including the loft spcae, with no rival signals of anything like
similar strength to
contend with. The only place where it goes below -70 Dbm is the ground floor
bathroom, which is physically farthest away, but even there I didn't
experience any
noticeable lag accessing various  websites (not that I plan to do that much
from the bathroom anyway).

Outside it is the strongest signal even 2~3 houses in either
direction, though that's
probably due the way the houses are wired, as the telephone socket is
well inside on the
ground floor which is where most people will put their routers I
guess. Whereas I
got NTT to put the cable into my study on the upper storey, which
means the router
ended up at the front near a window.
