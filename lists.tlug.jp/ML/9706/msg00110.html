I have just succeeded in installing the Caldera desktop in my machine, 
over a RedHat installation (we had tried to do this during the last 
meeting, but didn't know which packages were necessary).

Those who grabbed the OpenLinux Lite CD-ROM, here's the recipe:

	1) open the Redhat package management tool (glint)
	2) configure it to point to /cdrom/col/install/xappl1
	3) install these three packages:
		lg-eval-1.05-2.i386.rpm 
		lg-pg-1.0-5.i386.rpm
		lg-rules-1.1-1.i386.rpm 
	4) if you login as root, just launch "lg"
	   the configuration files are under /root/lg

LG is the Looking Glass Desktop interface. The version included 
in the CD-ROM is a 90-days evaluation version.

Another highlight of this distribution is the CRISP LITE, a graphical
text editor, but I didn't install it yet.
