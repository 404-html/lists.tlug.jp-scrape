  I d-e-t-e-s-t those damn NECs! We have a new 9821 machine which my
  son wanted (peer-compatability pressure :-)). I tried for months to
  get that machine to talk on our HAN, use SCSI disks and communicate
  via the built-in modem; not to mention load Linux, of course.

  I finally managed to get it to boot the `special' PC98 version of
  FreeBSD, but I've never seen an equivalent PC98 Linux. The ethernet
  interface and SCSI disks worked under FreeBSD and, with advice from
  Tim and a couple of other folks, I was eventually able to get it onto
  the HAN under W95J, too. I've given up completely on the built-in
  modem. :-(

  Porting Linux to the 98? Include me out!
