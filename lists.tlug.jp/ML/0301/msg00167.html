I just came across the "MoviX" Linux distro, whose sole purpose is playing DVDs and other multimedia.
http://movix.sourceforge.net/
It's an (20MB) ISO that you download and burn to a CD, then boot from.  By default, it doesn't need/touch your HD or network, though it *can* if you want.  It goes right into X and opens a media player. Then you swap that disk for your movie disk and play.  No region issues from what I saw. (I tried both region 1 and 2, no problems.)

If you are making your own movies, you can make a movie CD or DVD with MoviX on it that you just boot from and it will play.

I think it's a pretty neat idea - rather than doing tech support for your family, you can just mail them a disk of your home movies and tell them to boot from it if they have trouble.
