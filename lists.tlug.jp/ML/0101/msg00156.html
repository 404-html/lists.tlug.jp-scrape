You could try WXG:

http://www.aisoft.co.jp/japanese/ainews/news/freewxgl.asp

Quote from this page:

WXG> ●動作確認済み OS：
WXG> 　以下の OS 上で、WXG サーバの動作を確認しています。
WXG> 
WXG> 　■Linux
WXG> 　・Red Hat Linux 5.2J / 6.0J / 6.1 改訂 日本語
WXG> [...]

So it might run out of the box on your RedHat 5.2 system.

WXG is compatible to Canna, i.e. it speaks the same protocol.
Clients like kinput2, Mule, XEmacs, canuum work together with
WXG in the same way as they do with Canna. You will notice
almost no difference if you are running WXG instead of Canna,
only that the conversion quality is somewhat better.
Everything else works the same.

You can download WXG from http://www.ekotoba.com, but only a binary,
no source. And you cannot redistribute it.

I tried WXG on SuSE 7.1 and it works well.  But as it is not free
software, I am still using mainly Canna.
