Yes; I meant choice of IMAP server, not IMAP vs. POP.

I think Pine does this right.  But not all alleged IMAP clients do.
For example, fetchmail does not (and says so right on the box;
fetchmail assumes you want to manage your stream locally, that's why
you're injecting it into SMTP in the first place---fetchmail addresses
the converse problem of too many servers feeding the same user).

    Frank> And I am not going to get into the game of trying to
    Frank> persuade my users (whom I never see in one place at one
    Frank> time) to use a mail client that is not aglow with the halo
    Frank> of popularity.

At the present time you are limited to Pine, then; other IMAP clients
are not well known.  I haven't investigated much, though, since none
of my preferred Emacs MUAs implement IMAP, I don't use it.
