Well, boneheaded morons are a feature of every country, but what I'm
talking about are slightly higher-level users - people that normally use
computers and don't do things that are obviously stupid.  In Japan, these
people seem to want more personal attention, as opposed to reading the
manual.  And, this being Japan, it would be considered impolite to tell
the customer, "Ok, stack all the parts on your chair... now stand on top
so that you can see over your cubicle wall... now yell, "Does anyone here
know how to read a manual??"".. (taken from Dilbert).
