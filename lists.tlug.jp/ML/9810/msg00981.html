John De Hoog wrote:
> "Standards really do not come from committees. That's not how a single
> good standard has ever come about. It's market share forcing something
> to be used."
> -- Linus Torvalds, in a LinuxWorld interview
> http://www.linuxworld.com/linuxworld/lw-1998-10/lw-10-torvalds.html

Hence the great need to convince everyone to follow a standard that 
makes sense.  i.e. Alt tags so that non-conventional / graphical
browsers
can make sense of pages.  Standards are only worth anything if people
follow them.  If people follow them, they are worth a lot.  Many good
standards have died because consumers (via buying / other choices)
refused
to comply.
