Hi there,
Although you dislike the 1366x768 resolution, I would still recommend the Acer Aspire 1410/1810 series. Specifically, I bought this one last september :
http://www.amazon.com/Acer-Aspire-AS1410-8414-11-6-Inch-Sapphire/dp/B002LEXA64 (yes I know amazon.us doesn't ship laptops to japan, but its a nice reference)
I bought mine last year, bumped up the ram to 4 gigs and put in an ssd drive. Its cheap and fedora supports 99.999% of the hardware out of the box. The only thing that doesnt seem to work is the built-in mic, but who cares. 

Pros:
- around $400-450 USD new for the model I bought. some of the ones with a little bit higher specs are somewhat more expensive, but still way cheaper than a lets note 
- actual core 2 cpu, not some shitty castrated atom.
- 3 pounds, 11 inches
- real world usage of 4.5 hours (many firefox instances, wifi or emobile-usb-dongle for internet, possibly streaming music) - much better keyboard than the EEE-pc 1000 that I used to have (i'm talking to you, shitty right shift key thats the same size as and positioned adjacent to the up arrow key) 
- wifi works out of the box in fedora
- webcam works out of the box in fedora
- sound works out of the box in fedora

cons:
- the jp models ive seen have the celeron processors (sigh) so you'd have to buy one online from the US - built-in mic doesnt seem to work with fedora, perhaps ubuntu might work (ymmv but I dont think most people care about that functionality) 
- my model doesnt have bluetooth, but other updated versions do.
I bought mine in september of last year, but I have seen newer versions that have a core 2 duo cpu (mine has a core 2 solo, but it still beats the hell out of atom processors). Furthermore, newer models are shipping with 4 gigs of ram, and very soon now, acer will be releasing one with a core i5 cpu. 

just my 2 yen

-dave
