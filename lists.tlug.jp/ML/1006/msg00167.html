 > If Japanese planning bureaucrats do care for aesthetics, it doesn't

Agreed.

 > Except for some areas in Kyoto and (too) few tourist spots

 > like. Use and maximum dimensions are regulated (leading to the
 > weird slanted buildings you see so often), but not much else.
