Use Alladin GhostScript to convert ps to PDF (Portable Document Format),
they can then use Adobe Acrobat on MS Windows 95/NT or Apple Macintosh
to open it up.  There are also versions of Adobe Acrobat for other
major platforms such as Linux.  If your UNIX does not have Adobe Acrobat,
you can use XPDF or gs.

Adobe Acrobat is in wide use on Macintosh and Windows platforms
and I've found that most people that can use e-mail can get
the Acrobat reader.  The newer versions can also do searching.
Japanese versions are available.  Pictures and fonts are
awesome..  I use the times package of LaTeX to use PostScript
fonts.  

I always get praise from Mac and Windows people on the quality
of the documents I send them.  Nothing is lost in cross
platform translation.
