> I doubt that AOL will be releasing a Linux version of their user

Interesting that you mention them, because while they haven't released
their complete user interface, they are distributing their "AOL Instant
Messenger" (AIM) in a variety of forms including Java (complete with Linux
installation instructions).

AIM is very similar to ICQ.  It is a small application that can tell
you when your friends are connected to their providers (and have started
AIM) and provides a quick way to exchange text "chat" messages with them.
Sort of an amalgamation of finger and talk, ala IRC.

AIM is free, but carries with it a small advertising billboard.  Careful
window placement is advised.  :-)

http://www.aol.com/aim/home.html
