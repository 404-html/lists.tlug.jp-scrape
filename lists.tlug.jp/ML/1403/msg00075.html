I think Stephen already phased it very well.

Just as you want to use your laptop to show him they have a rightful
place in a library, you might want to leave the laptop at home and try
to read some books for a change.  *If* most library patrons really
needed to bring a laptop but can't, then they would stop going to the
library.  The library would have no one coming and they would have to
consider changing their policy.  Clearly, the number of people that
enters the library affects how much funding they get...if no one is
using it, then their existence isn't justified.  With the current
policy in place, they get enough customers...and, at least for the
moment...customers to apparently prefer to read.

Also, no one said computers are not useful for collaborative research;
more than likely, the library provides computers to search their card
catalogue and the web...  The point is about bringing your own
computer.
