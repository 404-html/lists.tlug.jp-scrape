>> I have several print dictionaries, including a large J-E Kenkyusha, but

The big Kenkyusha JE, known in the translator community as the Green
Goddess, had some wondefully archaic and at times mangled English in
both the translations and examples. The new 5th edition (aka GG5) came
out in 2003 and is a vast improvment over the 30yo GG4. Over half the
editors listed up the front are NSoEs (although many of those joined
late for the polishing.) Tom Gally, who I mentioned previously, worked
on it, and wrote some very good articles about it. See:

	http://www.gally.net/leavings/01/0112.html
	http://www.gally.net/leavings/01/0134.html
	http://www.gally.net/leavings/00/0095.html

The GG5 is available on CD and online. I subscribe to the online
edition (about Y550/mo). It has the advantage of being tapped in to
the current database.
