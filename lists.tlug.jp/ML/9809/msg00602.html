>period.)  Second, are their any recommendations for good

I have a Quantum 3.2 that has been in continuous operation in a non-air
conditioned environment since January, with no problems at all.  I consider it
to be a good, reliable disk.  There is, of course, no such thing as a disk
that cannot fail.  To that extent it's a crap shoot what you buy.

My computer at work has a 2.1 GB Quantum (a Fireball, as is the one in my home
machine) that has been in continous operation for about 1.5 years.

My other disk is a three-year old Western Digital Caviar 1.2 GB that has also
been in continous operation for most of that time.  It's also good and
reliable, although some people have told me that current WD drives are not as
good as they were when mine was new. 

IBM drives are also supposed to be pretty good.  They're certainly big and
fast.
