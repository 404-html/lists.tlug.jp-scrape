Hello,
I'm using gnus with Emacs 20.3.  There appears to be three ways
to get Japanese to appear with gnus:
  1) gnus without modification,
  2) gnus-mime,
  3) semi-gnus with APEL and SEMI.

I can read Japanese with 1 and 3.  I have not tried 2.  However, I
can't get the article summary buffers to decode MIME subject headers
when I just use gnus.  When I use semi-gnus the subjects are decoded
in the buffer.

When I use gnus, I can get the MIME encoded subject to display
in the article body with gnus-article-show-mime (I think).  However,
it still doesn't display in the summary buffer and I can't find
a command to decode just the headers.  

My question is if it is possible to read the MIME summary headers 
with just gnus or gnus-mime?  Or, do I have to use semi-gnus?  I'm
happy with semi-gnus.  However, I was just curious about how well
gnus alone handled Japanese.

REgards,
Craig
