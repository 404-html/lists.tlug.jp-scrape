 > Hello,

 > 	After upgrading the BIOS to support a new hard drive in my
 > ThinkPad 760/EL, I finally got TL 2.0j running. The Turbo install made
 > it all quite painless. Thanks Scott & Co.
 > 
 > The next step is getting it talking to the rest of the world. I have a
 > Aiwa PV-JE3610 modem/ethernet PCMCIA card that I would like to
 > use. Does anyone know where I can get a driver for this? I searched
 > around the web, but couldn't find anything. I have serious doubts that
 > such a beast exists...
 > 
 > If not, I'm open to suggestions as to a good card to get. I know a3Com
 > Etherlink III will work, but if there is something cheaper that works,
 > I'd rather save my money.
 > 
 > Thanks for your help.
 > 
 > Andy
Take a look at
http://hyper.stanford.edu/~dhinds/pcmcia/SUPPORTED.CARDS
There you can find all the known supported cards. The cheapest would
be to get a ne2000 compatible card. Maybe the Aiwa is ne2000
compatible. In that case you have to edit the /etc/pcmcia/config file
so that the card is recognized. The PCMCIA-HOWTO that comes with the
pcmcia-cs package explains how to do that. It can also be found at
http://hyper.stanford.edu/HyperNews/get/pcmcia/home.html

Hope that helps

Marcus
