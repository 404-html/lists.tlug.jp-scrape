This DM directive is empty in my /etc/sendmail.cf, but I use

  (setq user-mail-address "mike.fabian@example.com")  
  (setq mail-default-reply-to "mike.fabian@example.com") 

in my .emacs and this seems to set the "From:" and "Reply-to" fields
in my mail headers correctly.

Whether the local address works as an return address also depends on
the sendmail configuration of you provider. In my case you can send me
mail to mike@example.com (even to
"anything"@example.com) and it will arrive, although
"laputa" is only the name of my local machine.

In the /etc/sendmail.cf I use an entry for a "Smart" relay host:

DSsmtp-ma.rhein-neckar.de

This  is  supposed to have  the advantage  that a  mail which can't be
delivered immediately for  whatever reason (maybe because the  machine
of the  recipient is down) doesn't  stay in my  mail queue  but rather
moves on to the mail queue of my provider. The sendmail of my provider
then keeps trying to send the mail while I am offline again.
