Gregory Tucker writes:
 > Hi,
 > 
 > I am trying to use the digital camera SmartMedia with Linux (RedHat 6.1,
 > PCMCIA-cs-3.1.4) using the PC Card interface. It looks like it the mount
 > is failing on the ide module. I have included the relevant parts from
 > /var/messages.
 > 
 > Has anyone made this work properly? If so, this looks like it could be
 > just a resource conflict. 
 > 
 > Regards - g
 > -----
 > Jan 23 09:28:46 vega cardmgr[364]: initializing socket 1
 > Jan 23 09:28:46 vega cardmgr[364]: socket 1: ATA/IDE Fixed Disk
 > Jan 23 09:28:46 vega cardmgr[364]: executing: 'insmod
 > /lib/modules/2.2.12-20/pcmcia/ide_cs.o'
 > Jan 23 09:28:48 vega kernel: odd IO request: num 0010 align 0001 
 > Jan 23 09:29:05 vega kernel: ide_cs: ide_register() at 0x100 & 0x10e,
 > irq 9 failed 
 > Jan 23 09:29:06 vega cardmgr[364]: get dev info on socket 1 failed:
 > Resource temporarily unavailable
 > Jan 23 09:29:18 vega cardmgr[364]: shutting down socket 1
 > Jan 23 09:29:18 vega cardmgr[364]: executing: 'rmmod ide_cs'
It seems that you can't get the irq for the driver module. Either you
don't have enough irqs left, or there is another driver on irq 9 that
doesn't like to share. You should try to configure the
/etc/pcmcia/config.opts so that you exclude irq 9 and see what
happens. If you don't have enough irqs left, you can start the pcmcia
controller in polling mode instead of using an irq of its own. This
should be mentioned in the PCMCIA-HOWTO document. I am not sitting at
my linux notebook right now, or would send you my config files.

If the above doesn`t work, it could be a timing problem. You should
read PCMCIA-HOWTO to work those out.
