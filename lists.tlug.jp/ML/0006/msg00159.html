Unfortunately, it is an issue.  I gave Qmail a very hard look when it
was labelled `Gamma' and the opinion I got then was the Dan was adament
against fixing what I and others considered defects in the software.
Since then, the stuff I objected to the most has been corrected, but I've
never gone back and revisited switching to it again.

Personally, I don't like Qmail's un-Free/anti-Linux license.  Your
mileage may vary.

Since the IDA/M4 macro stuff got merged into sendmail, it has been a lot
easier to manage.  Security isn't particularly an issue on a personal
computer (as versus a company webserver or an ISP).  Forbid incoming
network connections to it and copy incoming mail via something like
fetchmail.
