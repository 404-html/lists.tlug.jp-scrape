             T L U G - Tokyo Linux Users Group
           ---------------------------------------

Meeting information **Please note change in location**

Date:  July 14 (Saturday)
Time:  13:30 - 16:30
Place: Akasaka Kumin Center
       4-18-13 Akasaka
       Minato-ku, Tokyo

(http://www.minatocity-kissport.or.jp/sisetu/akasaka/akasaka.htm)

*Many thanks to LinuxProbe/Security DNA for arranging a meeting place.

LinuxProbe (http://www.linuxprobe.org) is an organization working
to independently test and certify hardware and software for Linux,
providing unbiased information to serve the Linux community.

Topics:

1. Linux on a Compaq iPAQ

Jim Tittsler will talk about installing Linux on a Compaq iPAQ. Included
in his talk will be demonstration as well as a discussion of embedded 
Linux.

2. Linux Professional Institute (Japan)

Kinichi Kitano will talk about LPI in Japan including how to register for
the LPI examination and how to take the exam. He'll also talk about
what other kind of linux certification is available (Sair, A++...)


3. Red Hat Certified Engineer (Japan)

Scott Hughs will not be able to make it but will come and talk in more
detail at a future meeting.

4. Auction

There will be many items for auction including some brand new hardware.
If you have something you would like to donate to TLUG for auction THIS
is the time to do it. 


All Linux users and supporters of open source code and free software
are invited to attend this Tokyo Linux Users Group (TLUG) meeting.
Membership is open to anyone.  There are no membership dues or
entrance fees.

After the meeting, we will drink a few cold beers.


About TLUG
----------
TLUG is a non-profit, non-political organization established in June
of 1994 to exchange information on the use of Linux and Free Software
Foundation tools.  TLUG has an active mailing list, home page, and
regular physical meetings.  Discussions are primarily held in English,
but non-English speakers are encouraged to come and participate.
We especially want to encourage Japanese people to attend and will
make efforts to support a bilingual meeting.

TLUG hosts technical meetings every odd month on the 2nd Saturday, and
nomikai's every even month on the 3rd Friday.

The TLUG home page is available at http://tlug.gr.jp

If you are coming to the meeting, join the mailing list by sending
e-mail to majordomo@example.com including

   subscribe tlug
   end

in the body of the message.  You can get a feeling for what the group
is like by looking at the mail on the mailing list.

We look forward to seeing you there!
