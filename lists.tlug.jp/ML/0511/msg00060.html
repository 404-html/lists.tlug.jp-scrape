> In intercultural communication then we have a mismatch where Japanese

Very true. I was quite astonished to find that, when on the phone, if
I did not provide constant feedback (e.g. hai, hai, un, sou desu ka,
etc.), the Japanese person on the other end was likely to interrupt
himself, "moshi moshi?" Wondering if I was still on the line. :)

I have heard it said among gaijin that you know you have been in Japan
too long when you find yourself bowing on the phone. I am certainly
guilty of this. :)
