It means the engineers and scientists are not always writing technical
documents. I believe it's stil less ambiguity in scientific journals.
If it is not the article in the journals, most of the Japanese
documents are written to get the budgets, or for the advertizements.
That means the writer expects that the readers are not the engineers
or the scientists. 

In schools and discussion among engineers are still required to be
accurate and we're educated to use accurate words to use "IJOU" as the
meaning of "greater than or equal to". It's still common. So please
don't worry too much. :-)

And also sometimes the writers are avoiding some direct expressions to
express the politeness, as stated by some people here.
