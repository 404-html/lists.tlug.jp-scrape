When SATA drive is not connected,Linux will consider the
RAID device as primary drive. I think here the problem
is that linux couldn't find the driver for your RAID controller 
(only possible block device). 

Anyway, in addition to the boot disk, RH created seperate driver disks
for rare block/net/PCMCIA devices seperately. Ttry your lick with
those additional block device driver disk.btw, if you had created 
CD-ROMs from RH's iso images, then the first CD should be a
bootable one (?)
Most of the common vendors network cards are supported (other
than some domestic vendors latest/cheapest (like buffallow, corega),
check out RH's site for the supported network cards in RHL9.

whether it's installed on the SATA or RAID. Presumably another
