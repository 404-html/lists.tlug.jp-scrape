On 17/11/06, Josh Glover <jmglov@example.com> wrote:

This is the beginning of the end of the discussion on what to do with
the mailing list archives.


And here opens the voting.

I. The Issue

Our list archives are currently password protected.

II. Proposed Solutions

1. Do nothing
2. Open the archives, from the beginning of time to the end of it
(excluding tlug-admin and hikari)
3. Open the archives as of 01 January, 2007, but keep 2002-2006
password protected (again, excluding admin lists)

In the case of (2) and (3), email addresses will be munged. We will
most likely obscure everything after the '@' in the address.

In the case of (2), I will honour requests, on a thread-by-thread or
user-by-user basis, to remove content from the archives for the period
of 2002-2006 *only*.


Now that you understand the options, please vote.

Voting opens immediately, and runs until 2006/12/18 (three days after
I had originally scheduled it, since we are getting a late start on
the voting).

Your options are (1), (2), or (3), as detailed above. No write-ins or
commentary will be considered at this time.

You may vote either publicly, by replying to this email (please trim
out all of my post and simply record your vote), or privately, by
emailing me like this:

To: Josh Glover <jmglov@example.com>
Cc: Zev Blut <zb@example.com>
Subject: TLUG ML Archives Vote

I will announce the results on 2006/12/19.

Cheers,
Josh
