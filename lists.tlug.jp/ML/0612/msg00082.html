With regards to JavaScript-based address protection, I can report that I've been using a variation of this approach. I don't heavily mung the address, but the crucial JavaScript that generates the actual email address is in a separate .JS file that would require more clever crawling to access. I've been using that technique for many years now, and that particular email address receives very little spam, even though it's an old one.

However, some spam started appearing about a year ago, and my guess is that at least some of the spammers are getting even more cunning about their searches. With my technique, I think they would actually need to simulate the execution of the JavaScript and then crawl the entire tree to find it--it isn't even displayed in the rendered pages, but is inside a mailto: link. Thinking of it that way, it's perhaps more likely that the email address leaked from one of the very few people who have received email from it...

Anyway, I guess the main point remains that you can't solve economic problems with technical bandaids.
