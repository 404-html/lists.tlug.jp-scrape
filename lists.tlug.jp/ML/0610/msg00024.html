As large disks are not much of a problem anymore (save for
the 128/132GB limit), the document hasn't been updated
for a long time. But you can assume that most the workarounds
needed for older kernels are not necessary anymore.

I assume that the only problem you will have is to first
load a kernel. If you are past that step, then the kernel
will take care of anything else.

I suggest you to use grub instead of lilo, as its way of
accessing the disk is less dependend on the bios than lilo's
way of doing it (actualy, there is IMHO no good reason left
for using lilo unless you are really thight on space and
need every kByte you can get).
