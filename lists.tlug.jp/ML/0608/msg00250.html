I'd have to agree with this- my server was under 20 man, but then again, 
no bells or whistles. You can easily spend 40 man on a server, but why buy 
a ferrarri for in town travel? 

One other thing, oftentimes you will hear people saying "server class hardware", but take that with a 
grain of salt- one of our Dell servers here at work had 3 scci drives fail 
in a raid5 array simultaneously. It had these cheap seagate drives that 
are prone to failure in my experience. So just because you build it 
yourself doesn't inherently make it any less reliable than something built 
by a major OEM: on the contrary sometimes manufacturers cut corners that 
you wouldn't. Also, here at work I built 6 out of 12 of our servers, 
and haven't had any problems with any of them (knock on wood)- a few have 
been online for 4 years now. They were 5 times less expensive than if I 
had purchased a prebuilt server at same specs.

I agree with going for quality RAM - it does make a difference, but 
sometimes it is hard to find in Akihabara. I went to 6 different stores 
before I could track down the RAM I was looking for (ECC registered), I 
eventually found it in a store that sells gaming stuff by Users side.

Anyway good luck with it. 

Cheers,

Scott VanDusen
