scott wrote:

Hi everyone,

I'm trying to decide which direction to go with my Zaurus C3200. I'm
currently using a cacko rom, but I cant find a version of the ko/pi
synch rpm compatible with centos4.
Ouch, there's nothing in freshrpms.net or any of the other RPM repositories available? I'm afraid I don't use Centos 4 that much so am not really sure.
You might be able to get the source tarball and compile it on your own and then use checkinstall [1] to create a RPM so it can easily be installed or uninstalled on Centos machines. 



I haven't tried open zaurus as of yet- still a bit wary of having to
repartition the hard drive because if it turns out to be a huge hassle
getting everything to work it might not be worth it.
OZ has been making progress but if you want a stable PDA I'm not sure OZ is at that stage yet. I could be utterly wrong though. 



Any recommendations for synching my PIM data? Should I just repartition
and see what happens?
If you can get KO/PI up and running, I have found that to be your best option. It might be worth the hassle of trying to compile KO/PI on Centos4 to get a pretty nice PIM suite.
My brief attempts to use the Sharp one left such a bad taste in my mouth (proprietary format, poorly documented, UTF-8 un-friendly) I consider the included PIM suite an anti-feature personally.
I also setup my KO/PI setup to sync over SSH to my main server which is actually rather nice although it does necessitate having a network connection but that should be a piece of cake with the Zaurus ;-) 


Alain
