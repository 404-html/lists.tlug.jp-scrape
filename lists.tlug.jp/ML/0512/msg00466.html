On 21/12/05, Jim <jep200404@example.com> wrote:

> Advance Programming in the UNIX Environment (aka APUE), Second Edition
> by W. Richard Stevens and Stephen A. Rago
> is a superbly written serious book

I would go so far as to say that a UNIX programmer should not be
without the W. Richard Stevens bookshelf, including:

- APUE
- UNPv1 (UNIX Network Programming, Volume 1: The Sockets Networking API) [1]
- UNPv2 (UNIX Network Programming, Volume 2: Interprocess Communications) [2] *
- TCP/IP Illustrated, Volume 1: The Protocols [3]
- TCP/IP Illustrated, Volume 2: The Implementation [4] **

* Not "necessary", just *highly* recommended
** Serious TCP stack kernel hackers only

Cheers,
Josh
