Playing with Ubuntu the other day, I found that both it and Mepis are a
bit more difficult to get working than some others.  I wound up having
some success by using a scim-anthy port--on my
http://www.scottro.net/jpninpt.html I have a link to it in the Deb
section, where I say go to the scim-anthy page and do a search for
Ubuntu.

Add the suggested sources from the link to /etc/apt/sources.lst or
whatever it's called.  First uninstall scim, anthy and any of the uim
stuff you put in.

Then, 

apt-get update

You'll get a warning that the sources can't be verified or something.
Then, apt-get install scim-anthy which should put in a copy of scim and
and anthy while it's doing its thing. 

Then,

export XMODIFIERS="@example.com=SCIM"
export LC_CTYPE=ja_JP.utf8

Hit ctrl+space and see if you get any joy in a uxterm. 

Good luck.  :)
