Jim Breen writes:
 > Well, thanks everyone. I now have scim/anthy
 > working fine on the EeePC, and can input Japanese
 > into apps like Firefox, vi, etc.
 > 
 > What I had to do was set up a terminal with a Japanese
 > environment. It may have been overkill, but as root I did:
 > XMODIFIERS=@example.com LC_ALL=ja_JP.UTF-8 LANG=ja_JP.UTF-8 uxterm&

According to POSIX, LC_ALL overrules LANG; if you need both, something
is broken.  It's been a while since I needed to care, but I believe
that LC_ALL actually overrules all the specific ones (LC_CTYPE,
LC_TIME, etc).  I mention this just so you have a fighting of
diagnosing a bug where LC_CTYPE or something doesn't DTRT in your
uxterm.

But congratulations on getting things working!
