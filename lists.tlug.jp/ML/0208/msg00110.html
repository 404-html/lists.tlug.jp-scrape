On Wed, Aug 07, 2002 at 11:42:40AM -0400, Josh Glover wrote:

>Let's face it, Windows sysadmins are easier to come by, especially for
>companies that are willing to hire anyone with an MSCE.

Cheap Windows admins are easier to come by, but you get what you
pay for.  A really good NT/2K/XP admin is worth quite a bit,
and all other things being equal, the challenges to remote
administration that Windows presents may also mean that you need
more admins.  So even if you have one or two senior ones and
a bunch of shake-n-bake MCSEs under them, the total cost of staff
may be higher than having one or two senior *nix admins and a few
junior and mid-level ones.
