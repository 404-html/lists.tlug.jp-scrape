-=-=-=- Announcement: 2008-10-08 TLUG Technical Meeting -=-=-=-=-

  Meeting information

  Date: November 8 (Saturday)
	
  Time: 14:00 - 16:30
        (doors open 13:30)

  Place: SUN Microsystems, Inc.
         SBS Tower, 27th floor
         4-10-1 Yoga
         Setagaya-ku, Tokyo, Japan

     *** note the change from the usual venue ***

  Details:
http://www.tlug.jp/wiki/Meetings:Locations:Yoga:Sun

  Main Topic)

         "Serving dynamic webpages in less then a millisecond"
         By John Fremlin

  Discussion)

         "designing and building high-performance HTTP systems"

  Lightening Talk)

         "Replacing the page cache with varnish[1]"
         By Edward Middleton
	
This month, John Fremlin will introduce the web application platform he
developed. The talk will cover issues like the greatest contributors to
latency and how he dealt with them, as well as other issues he addressed
in performance tuning.

The talk will be followed by a discussion of the design of high
performance HTTP systems.

Finally, Edward Middleton will give a lightening talk about how he used
varnish to replace the page cache in his typo web blog[2].

If you are involved in a project or have something you would like to
talk about, we are always looking for presenters.

Other items on the agenda are:
1. Introduction of new members, news about Linux/Open Source,
general announcements, Q&A
2. Auction

All Linux users and supporters of open source code and free software
are invited to attend this Tokyo Linux Users Group (TLUG) meeting.
Membership is open to anyone. There are no membership dues or entrance
fees.

After the meeting, we will continue chatting over a few beers.

About TLUG
----------
TLUG is a non-profit, non-political organization established in June
of 1994 to exchange information on the use of Linux and Free Software
Foundation tools. TLUG has an active mailing list, home page, and
regular physical meetings. Discussions are primarily held in English,
but non-English speakers are encouraged to come and participate.
We especially want to encourage Japanese people to attend and will make
efforts to support a bilingual meeting.

TLUG generally hosts technical meetings every odd month on the 2nd
Saturday, and a nomikai (social even at a pub) every even month on the
2rd Friday.

The TLUG home page is available at http://www.tlug.jp
