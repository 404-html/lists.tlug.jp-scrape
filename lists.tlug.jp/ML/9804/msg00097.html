That's another, although T-Zone has more Linux books and UNIX books, I
think.  Another alternative is to order from Amazon.com, which will be
cheaper even with shipping if you're ordering more than about two books.

I'd also like to recommend the book "Running Linux", published by O'Reilly
(authors = Matt Welsh and Lar Kaufman).  The first 2 or 3 chapters are a
little dated with respect to modern versions of Linux (the book is from
1996, which means it was probably written in 1995, which makes it a ancient
by Linux standards), but most of it remains a very valuable source.  Even
the first part is good as background reference.
