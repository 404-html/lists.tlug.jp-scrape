On Wed, 8 Apr 1998, joem wrote:

> 
> > Just in case anyone is looking for a Linux-compatible 4x CDR drive -
> > I've found one.  The Yamaha CDR-400t.  You need cdrecord 1.5 to use it
> > (freely available on sunsite).  What's really interesting is that the
> > CDR manual says that you need to use it with an Adaptec 2940 SCSI card,
> > although that might just be a marketing agreement w/ Adaptec :)
> 
> This is a good drive ... we did a demo of it at the last TLUG meeting
> using Xcdroast.  I was using a Buslogic 946c adapter, and had no problems.

so the cdwrite binary that comes with Xcdroast will handle 4x burns?
Interesting.  I thought only cdrecord handled 4x burns.

cdrecord, IMHO, is much better than cdwrite.  has some really nice
features, plus it supports more drives.  I may someday make a perl/Tk
frontend for it (kind of like xcdroast...), but I'm swamped right now :)
