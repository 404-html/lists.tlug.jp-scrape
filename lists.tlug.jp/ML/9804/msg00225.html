On Tue, 14 Apr 1998, Martos-Riano wrote:

> The whole thing is that I am not able to configure the ne2000  Ethernet card.
> 
> the make menuconfig includes (not with a M of module but with a * of inclusion)
> the three modules needed for having the ethernet card running, the Ethernet
> module, the ne2000 module, and a third module for internetworking (the ones I
> was told on saturday).
> 
> After compiling the kernel and booting with it, I can check again that dmesg
> doesn't display any message regarding an eth0 or anything similar.
> 
> Suggestions?

Build it as a module.  Then it's easy to specify commandline options to it
w/o having to reboot.  ISA ne2000 cards *always* need commandline options
with Linux.  build it as a module, boot, then do:

modprobe ne io=0x300

and see if that works.  Try other IOs until you get the right one.  Valid
ranges are 0x280-0x340, in steps of 0x020.
