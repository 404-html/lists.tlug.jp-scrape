TLUG,
I feel like I'm always shedding used electronics. Here's some more. One of the items I still think has some worth, but the rest are free. 

Sale:

Palm Pilot TX.
Unfortunately, I lost the USB connector cable, so that's a bit of an inconvenience. For the last year or so I've just been using it as my MP3 player and Japanese dictionary. Takes SD cards of up to 2GB, maybe more, not sure. Comes with install disks (for Windows - for Linux connectivity all software needed is available in repositories), recharger, 128MB SD Card, Manual, 2 unused protective covers, and a hard metal case. The metal case has taken a bit of a beating, so it's in sore shape. But on the upside, that's it's job, so the Palm device itself is in great condition. One thing about this unit, unlike most used Palm Pilots, is that the screen still works impeccably. No dead spots. Last I looked in Akihabara, these were going for around 10,000 yen. I checked on eBay, and it seems these go for about 80 bucks US. Since mine has no USB cable, I'm thinking 3000 yen? 


Free:
(Free means that you have to arrange to either come pick it up in Ebisu/Hiroo, or I'll send it to you COD/Chakubarai. I'll place things in whatever packaging I have lying around that might do the job. Oh, it also means no promises about... well, anything really. Priority goes to people who offer to take all the items.) 

Internal 3 1/4 slot size card reader.
Generic brand, connects by USB. Reads SD cards, Micro SD cards, Sony memory sticks, a bunch of other formats. I used it primarily for reading SD cards, but it can only take up to 1GB capacity, so I had to replace it. It only cost about 1500 yen new. But it's free for you if you want it. 

Phone.
Just a phone. A white one. Basic. Has numbers. Can make calls. It's nice. Brand new condition. Don't remember the last time I ever had a land line, and I'm not sure even why I have this. 

Sony PCG-XR7E/K Laptop.
It almost runs, but not quite. I wiped Windows off and tried installing Ubuntu, but the installer just wouldn't finish. I think it's the hard drive, but I wouldn't make any promises. It's an older model, has a PCI card Ethernet connection. Comes with charger. It's a little dusty and worn in places, but pretty good shape given it's age, I think. It wasn't mine - someone gave it to me saying I could keep it if I could make it work. Since it's beyond me to get any reaction out of it, maybe someone here would fare better.
