Sure.  So what?  If you want compensation for your work, I have no
problem with that.  I don't care if you use an appropriate non-free
license, such as a Creative Commons No-Commercial-Use or the Aladdin
Free[sic] Public License.  Or a full-blown closed proprietary license.
It's your choice.

If your interest is in getting the company to make a contribution to
society through your software, every customer they get that you
wouldn't have reached yourself is such a contribution.[1]  The fact
that the company also makes a profit is gravy on the social meatloaf.

 > Stallman he may have done a lot of good for those of us that use GPL

You are seriously confused if you think that before Stallman's brain
death (comments in the XEmacs sources notwithstanding, there's life in
that old bag of jelly yet) anything he doesn't approve of will get
into a published version of the GPL.  Stallman listens to the opinions
and expertise of others, and occasionally even hears them ;-), but he
decides.

 > I try to stick to LGPL libs. For this I do think Stallman is out of
