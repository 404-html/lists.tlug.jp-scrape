As always, thanks Scott.

This worked for me in Ubuntu.

First I installed Japanese language support.

Then, after installing scim, I added the following to my $HOME/.bashrc

export XMODIFIERS='@im=SCIM"
export LC_CTYPE=en_US.utf8



> Just for fun, you could try commenting out all the things you added, and
> see if adding those two lines to $HOME/.bashrc does any good.
> (You might also add export QT_IM_MODULE="scim")


So far, no luck. I do have the Japanese locale installed, and I also commented out the settings that I had made in my ~.i18r and X11/scim files. I'll keep digging around--this shouldn't be an unresolvable problem. One question, though. When you wrote this line

> export XMODIFIERS='@im=SCIM"

you probably mis-typed and used single quotes on one side and double on the other. Obviously it has to be matched, but does it matter which one I use?
