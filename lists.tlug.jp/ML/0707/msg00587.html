I agree. I get ticket at applications that do not let me control my
date formats irrespective of my locale. I prefer YYYY/mm/dd, not the
US's idiotic mm/dd/YYYY and the Euro Zone's unfortunate dd/mm/YYYY
(only unfortunate that the US pissed in the pool with their moronic
format). I set my locale to en_UK or _IE so that spell-checking works
the way I expect, but I like the JP dates: YYYY年mm月dd日. Just too bad

I personally like either dd/mm/YYYY or YYYY/mm/dd. to me these make
more sense to me as they progress from a smaller to larger unit of
measure in order (or decrease). The U.S. format makes not sense to me:
Months ---> days --> YEARS....
