That's because "better and worse" are subjective decisions. The idea
is to provide enough information so the prospective buyer can make his
subjective decision an informed one.

It is just like buying a car; maybe I want a sports car, so a Honda
Civic SI would better serve my needs than a Ford Expedition. But maybe
you hate humanity and want to bring about the end of the planet's
ability to sustain life; in which case the Expedition would be the
clearly better choice for you.

But both of us are well-served by a site that simply lists as much
technical information as possible about cars in general.
