On 2017-12-15 10:52 +0100 (Fri), Christian Horn wrote:

> one can register a single gmail account, i.e. user@example.com,
> and then becomes reachable under user+something@example.com .

Yes, that's quite handy. There are sites, however, that disallow `+`
signs in the username portion of email addresses; for these if you
really need to you can often bypass the restriction with arbitrary
dots in a username. I.e., `foo@example.com`, `f.oo@example.com` and
`fo.o@example.com` are all the same mailbox. (This has been handy for me
on some sites where I needed multiple accounts but they insisted on a
unique email address for each account and didn't allow `+`s.)
