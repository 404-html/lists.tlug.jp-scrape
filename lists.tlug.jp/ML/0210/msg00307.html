I've been wondering the same thing.  I've looked at the pages for it and of course it solves the problems of diminishing address space, but what else?
There's multicast, but I don't really have a need or desire to watch video over the web - I don't even watch TV...

Actually, some of the 'advantages' of IPv6 make me a bit uneasy - from a cursory glance, it looks like your machine is much more identifiable than if it were running that Intel processor ID from a while back.  I know that IPv4 isn't exactly anonymous, but at least I understand the implications a bit better.

Looking at the websites, I see a few HOWTO documents, but what I really wanted to see was a WHY document - what can I really do with it that I can't with v4?  Don't get the idea that I'm somehow against v6 - I really haven't formed an opinion.

Like a lot of people, I make do with one dynamic IP at home and I manage to do a *lot* with NAT, VPN and subnetting.  (Apache has the ability to serve up a site based upon the name by which it was called;  If routers could do NAT this way, instead of by port, I can't see that I'd ever need more than one IP address for home or a small site...) 

I think this would be an *excellent* topic for a technical meeting.  Anyone else?
(It looks like there was one back in '98 featuring IMASY - has anyone been using them for this? )

Cheers,
Jim
