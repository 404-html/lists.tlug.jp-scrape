As I remember, there was such a discussion here one year ago.

A major concern is an SSD can just die 'instantly', without approaching
its cycle life time. I've seen a customer with X25-M disks arrays having
dead SSDs after just one month and only few Gigabytes written/low write
ratio... and a high grade SLC PCIe card gone the same way.

The problem seems to be on how they use cells, not their type.
Intel & Crucial are both using micron flash memory for consumer drives,
and there's a reliability ratio of 1:5 between the two brands.
