On Tue, Apr 20, 2004 at 05:01:28PM -0400, Scott Robbins wrote:
> As mentioned earlier, suddenly, my fcc-hook in .muttrc stopped working.
> I had one that if a message went to mpolak, it should go in a certain
> folder.  
> 
> It turns out to have something to do with my headers.  I send most mail
> from nyc.rr.com or despammed.com.  However, when sending to mpolak, my
> header is starlofashions.com.  A bit more searching of google indicated
> an old problem that somehow, mutt is not recognizing the header as
> belonging to me.  
> 
> I haven't yet found out what I have to change to get it to do so, but
> thought I would post this discovery, anyway.


SOLVED

As of mutt 1.5.5 or so, I think, the set alternates="scott@example.com" no
longer worked, but was considered an unknown variable.  I commented it
out and never fixed it.  

Using the new syntax
alternates "scott@example.com|scott@example.com" solved the problem

I ~knew~ the answer had to be there somewhere.
