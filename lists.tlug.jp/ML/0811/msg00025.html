Just saying that, in 1995, session managers did indeed exist.  They were
primitive--they worked by running a shell script in your home directory
rather than by remembering the state of the applications that were
running when you last exited, but they certainly were there.

Come to think of it, HP-VUE (which startled me by showing up in Solaris
as CDE) first came out in 1989 or so, and it had all of the modern
session management stuff that you're wrestling with today.
