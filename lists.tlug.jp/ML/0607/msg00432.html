Stuart,

Hmmm. My understanding was that the PILOTRATE variable was relevant only
to serial connections.
From what I've read, you should be correct. And I can't say for absolute sure that changing the speed was the deciding factor in getting my Palm to connect. All I can say was I changed the speed setting, and the next attempt to sync succeeded. It might be coincidence, as there are clearly a lot of variables involved. 

And, more than variables, there is a lot of mysterious forces involved.
Before I said that gnome-pilot was very flaky. I'm going to change that qualifier to *too* flaky. One has to press the hotsync at just the right moment, hope that there hasn't been some kind of error, hope that the whole connect/reconnect sequence is in the right phase, and even when you get that miraculous synchronizing screen on the desktop, it's more likely to get part way through and crash than it is to work.
So I looked up gnome pilot on the web, to just generally see what the deal is. And apparently it's all messed up. If there's a proper FAQ or main development web site, let me know, 'cause I couldn't find it. What I could find is scatterd postings in mailing lists saying that the gnome-pilot development project has been beset by all sorts of problems. Mainly developers leaving the project and the ones that are still on are too busy with other things to really get to work on it.
I can't even find out for sure what the current status of it is, or even if it's really being currently actively developed. 

This doesn't fill me with confidence that I should really be using it.
What are my other options? J-pilot, Kpilot, pilotmanger, and pilot-link, so far as I know. Well, these don't seem very stable either. I've tried all of them, and I can't get any of them to connect. Or, to be more accurate, half the time I don't even know if I've successfully connected or not, or if the error output I get is because I've done something wrong or the application did.
In short, connecting via the palm seems to have been solved on a more base, Palm to OS level. But finding a half decent application to do anything with that connection seems to be a challenge.
If anyone has a recommendation for which they believe is the most stable, and why and how, I'm all ears. 

--
Dave M G
