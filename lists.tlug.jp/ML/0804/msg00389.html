On Monday 14 April 2008 21:43, CL wrote:
>One of the first issues I encounter trying to use Iceweasel is
>that you can't download add-ons or themes unless they are in *.xpi
>format.

I didn't realize extensions came in other formats; all the ones i use 
come as *.xpi. There are one or two that i can't install directly 
through the https://addons.mozilla.org site; i have to download first 
and then open them from the hard drive. But even those are *.xpi, and 
i've had to go through the download-and-then-open rigamarole on other 
operating systems too, so i don't think it is a Debian packaging issue.

>There is a particular Theme I prefer as it exactly accommodates all of

That sounds useful. Mind if i ask what theme it is?

>Iceweasel has a number of add-on programs included as standard, such

> for NoScript.

I didn't realize Iceweasel came with those extensions. I actually use 
both of them, but installed them myself into my profile. I installed 
them quite awhile ago though; perhaps it was before they were included 
as standard. I was under the (perhaps mistaken) impression, however, 
that very few extentions come bundled with the Iceweasel package 
itself. I have several extra iceweasel packages installed on my 
machine:
    iceweasel
    iceweasel-dom-inspector
    iceweasel-gnome-support
    iceweasel-greasemonkey
    iceweasel-webdeveloper
Most of those i could kill if i cared to; i use dom-inspector and

Greasemonkey is on my long-term todo list (meaning, by the time i get 
around to it the sun might have already burned out).
