Stephen J. Turnbull wrote:

Every bilingual body who had a choice stopped using ichitaro as soon
as a Japanese-capable Word was released on a Windows that didn't need
double booting.  I doubt that any other country has anything similar
to Ichitaro.

Actually there is an interesting case in Korea, where a combination of nationalistic sentiment and a hatred of Microsoft has led to a situation where the majority of Koreans use the homegrown word processor HWP (Hangul Word Processor--running on Windows, of course). I am still amazed by the fact that virtually everything my Korean colleagues send me is written in this.

See http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hangul_(word_processor)
