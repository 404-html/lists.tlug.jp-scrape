Some of you may notice that this mail is being sent from a WIN
machine.  That's because I have managed to break Kubuntu.  Well, maybe
break is a bit harsh ...

After an number of upgrade-created problems in going from 7.04 to 7.10
to 8.04, I reinstalled v.8.04 from a CD (the .iso came from one of the
main servers and not a mirror) without reformatting the partition in
hopes of being able to just drag and drop some of the pre-existing
structure into the new version.  Instead, I now have a version of
Kubuntu that offers absolutely no language support -- except English.
There are absolutely no other choices available.  There are other
problems, too.  The list of available packages on Adept Installer is
seriously shortened -- no Firefox, no Thunderbird, no Synaptic ... and
a lot of other recognizable stuff.  Apt-get commands result in "file
not found" responses even for stuff that is obviously there.

So, I reformatted the partition and reinstalled.  And, once again, I
have no language support.  I can't find anything in the manuals to
indicate that language support is even a separate, installable
package.  Adept says Language Support is installed.  I tried
uninstalling and reinstalling but, as soon as Language Support is
removed the icon in Adept is greyed out and it can't be reselected.

I'm trying to download an .iso   from a Japan site to see if I get
something different (file size of the .iso from JAIST is a different
size from the one I got from the main Ubuntu server).

Anyone else seen this kind of result?
