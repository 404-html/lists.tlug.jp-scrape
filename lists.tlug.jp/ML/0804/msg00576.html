SL Baur wrote:
Does Ubuntu support both the proprietary accelerated ATI driver and Japanese?
Kubuntu recognizes the X1300 chip on my desktop model even though I don't currently use it, and it has offered me the option of enabling or disabling the driver and full flgrx support on demand. Since the chip in question is on a first generation PCI card made by a maker I'd never heard of before acquiring the box, I switched to the on board Intel 865. Although, part of that may also be that the WIN "extras" disk I got with the card was so full of trojans and rootkits that I wondered about the wisdom of even running the card at all.

It also does Japanese. It installs SCIM when you select the language right from the Regionalization menus. I don't have it running to my satisfaction, but the problem is mine and not Kubuntu's. YMMV.
