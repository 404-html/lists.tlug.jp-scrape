>>>>> "Eric" == Eric Standlee <fwiw3980@example.com> writes:

    Eric> make[2]: *** Warning: Clock skew detected.  Your build may
    Eric> be incomplete.

This statement is incomplete in the sense that make only detects clock 
skew if there are times in the future.  Of course, the reverse is
probably not very dangerous.

    Eric> What is the easiest way around this?  I have reset the time
    Eric> in Bios and system time seems to be okay.

Do `ls -lt | head' and wait until the time in the top line ;-)

Alternatively, do

make clean

and try again.  If that doesn't work, do `ls -lta | less', then
preserve (by cp'ing to different files) anything important to you
that's dated in the future (in particular .config), do `make
mrproper', and cp the files back where they came from.  (Use cp, not
mv, because that sets the file timestamp to current time.  mv
preserves it, and preserves the problem.)
