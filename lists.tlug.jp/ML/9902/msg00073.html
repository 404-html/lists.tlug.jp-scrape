Okay, so about half a year back I was installing RH 5.1, and had a little
trouble using Xconfigurator to create my XF86config file. I came here to
TLUG, and Chris told me to make the following adjustments:

	(--) SVGA: PCI: Trident TGUI 96xx rev 211, Memory @ 0xfb800000, MMIO
@example.com
	(**) SVGA: chipset:  generic
	This is your problem.
	Remove the "chipset generic" line from your XF86Config

Cool. Then Matt recomended i use xf86config to configure my video card.
Armed with these gems of knowledge, I went to m machine and had it running
smoothly in no time.
So here it is six months later, and I'm setting up RH 5.2, and i runn into
the same problem again. "Aha!" Thinks I, "I'll just go to the TLUG archive,
get the same solution as before, and everything will be peachy".
So i go home and use xf86config to set up my XF86config file, and then I
remove the "chipset: generice" line. And my monitor goes haywire when I say
"startX". 
So after fiddling around, I discover that this time Xconfigurator has the
highest rate of success, because it won't give me a resolution higher than
320X200, but at least it doesn't turn my monitor into blithering idiot
(that's my job!)
Sigh.
So what can i try here?
_________

the Screen section, where, in the SVGA part, all the lines for my card have
been commented out. However, I tried uncommenting them and commenting out
