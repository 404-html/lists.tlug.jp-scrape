On Sat, 30 Nov 1996, C. Oda wrote:

> I'm trying to get cnprint to output to an HP LaserJet IV.  The
> printer has English PostScript fonts but not Japanese PostScript.
> Can I print to it? 

  You shouldn't need any Japanese fonts at the printer, since cnprint
takes care of making the Postscript image file that can then be printed on
( I would think) any Postscript printer or anything that ghostscript 
supports (like even my humble Canon BJC-400J).

> I get segmentation faults when I attempt to run cnprint on an EUC text file. 

  Take a look at the command-line options in Section V of CNPRINT.HELP.
cnprint correctly auto-detects Big5 and/or GB Chinese, but
I think you'll probably have to use the -euc switch to specify Japanese
EUC input ( I know I used the -jis switch to print my kanji files).
You may also want to use the -w switch to tell cnprint to just create the
Postscript file, without sending it to the printer.  You can then check it
first with ghostview, before sending it down in the usual way.

> I've also tried to save
> a Japanese Web Page as PostScript and then print it.  Both

  Netscape, at least, is not kanji-smart when it saves a page as Postscript.
