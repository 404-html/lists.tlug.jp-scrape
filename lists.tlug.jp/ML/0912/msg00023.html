         "Parallels Virtuozzo"

This month, Alexei Anisimov will introduce and demonstrate Virtuozzo[1], a

If you are involved in a project or have something you would like to talk

All Linux users and supporters of open source code and free software are
invited to attend this Tokyo Linux Users Group (TLUG) meeting.

the meeting you are still welcome to attend the Bonenkai.

About TLUG
----------
TLUG is a non-profit, non-political organization established in June of 1994
to exchange information on the use of Linux and Free Software Foundation
tools. TLUG has an active mailing list, home page, and regular physical
meetings. Discussions are primarily held in English, but non-English
speakers are encouraged to come and participate.
We especially want to encourage Japanese people to attend and will make
efforts to support a bilingual meeting.

TLUG generally hosts technical meetings every odd month on the 2nd Saturday,
and a nomikai (social even at a pub) every even month on the 2rd Friday.

The TLUG home page is available at http://www.tlug.jp
