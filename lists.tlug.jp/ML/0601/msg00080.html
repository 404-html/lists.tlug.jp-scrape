On Wed, Jan 11, 2006 at 09:41:58AM -0500, Jim wrote:
> Fedora held a survey about SCIM in November. 
> Why would Fedora hold such a survey? 
> My guess is that with Fedora as the beta for RHEL, 
> scim is under review to replace the incumbant IME 
> not only for Fedora, but also for RHEL (and hence Centos). 


At present, I think their default is still kinput2 and canna--if one
chooses Japanese support during installation, at least as of a few weeks
ago, they installed canna.

However, yum has scim, anthy and scim-anthy available, at least since
FC4.  (Not all of the offshoots have it yet though, apparently, e.g.
Blag.)

NetBSD now has anthy and uim, though if you don't know how to use
PKG_OPTIONS, it'll also install canna.  :)  I guess the venerable
kinput2 -canna combo is gradually passing away.
