I think that this is more of a reference to the style of the 
installation rather than the final result--you are still going to end 
up with a Knoppix Desktop.

You will get various opinions as to the merits of running Knoppix vs. 
pure Debian. I will sometimes install Knoppix on a computer (usually a 
laptop) for which I am having trouble getting some aspect of Debian 
(such as X) to install properly. Knoppix will usually recognize all of 
your hardware, even Wireless and so forth, and give you whatever 
screen resolution you want.

You may run into problems, however, if you try to update anything, or 
add anything, because there are various aspects of the Knoppix setup, 
such as file associations and permissions, that are Knoppix-specific, 
and a standard Debian upgrade will usually run into conflicts with 
Knoppix right away. Just today I had Knoppix on my Mebius MP50G and I 
wanted to install GFTP. When I tried to do this, apt automatically 
began to update a wad of files, and then crashed when trying to 
upgrade kbluetooth, which is a Knoppix-protected file. Other 
complications arose, and so I just wiped out Knoppix and reinstalled 
Sarge, which works pretty well, except for Wifi, and lack of access to 
some packages not yet in Sarge, such as partimage.

Anyhow, if you get Knoppix installed to your hard drive, you might 
want to copy some of its X config files, fstab, its Desktop, etc., 
which you can use in your Debian in the future.
