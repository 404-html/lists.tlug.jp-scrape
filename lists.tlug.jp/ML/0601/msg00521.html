I was very surprised when I started getting calls from 
East Asia by Skype instead of by POTS [1], that the 
latency with Skype was _lower_ than with POTS. 
Granted the POTS calls were probably made with calling 
cards for cheap rate, but it was nonetheless surprising 
that the latency was lower with Skype. 

----------------------------------------------------------------

I don't like the latency with either Skype or POTS, 
but I incorporate it into the other accomodations 
I make for non-native speakers, which are SSS.

   I talk SLOWLY, clearly pronouncing each word separately. 
   Instead of one big long masterpiece of a sentence, 
   I use several SHORT sentences instead. 
   Each sentence is SIMPLE, eschewing sophisticated nuanced 
   words and grammar. 

For latency, I wait a little longer before talking 
to avoid stepping on each other's toes. 

[1] POTS: Plain Old Telephone Service
