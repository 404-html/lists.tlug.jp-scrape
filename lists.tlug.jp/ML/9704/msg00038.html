    Totoro> to print the output file, I get a bunch of random

This suggests that the printer is somehow in "the wrong mode."  One
possibility is that (as you suspected) there is some kind of "Japanese
mode" for the J-version of the firmware.  More likely is that your
filter software is still getting in the way, somehow.  Try resetting
the printer, and doing "cat test.lj >/dev/printer" or "cat test.lj
>/dev/lp0" or whatever seems to be the printer device.  This will
probably not screw up your lpr daemons and stuff, because it bypasses
the filters and spooling completely.  And with your current problems
you're presumably not printing much of anything any other way....

If you're multibooting, you can try using DOS or windows to print the
test.lj file produced by Ghostscript.
