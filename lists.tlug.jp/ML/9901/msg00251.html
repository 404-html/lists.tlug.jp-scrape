    ash> The strange thing is that ldd says that it can find

>>>>> "Stephen" == Stephen J Turnbull <turnbull@example.com> writes:

    Stephen> `ldd' _finds_ _dependencies_, it does not _load_
    Stephen> _libraries_.

    ash> Well, isn't end result the same? I understood that ldd worked
    ash> by watching what libraries the program loads. I could be
    ash> completely wrong on this though...

No.  I'm not sure exactly what happens, but it's definitely not that.
As I understand it, ldd munges the environment of ld.so so that ld.so
sees an argc of 0, which recent (ie, in the lifetime of Scott Stone :-) 
ld.so special-case to report on libs used.

I don't know just how far ld.so goes in creating this report; maybe it 
loads the libraries, maybe it just reads enough of them to learn their 
dependencies.  But the conflicts that hose a library and result in
"can't load" messages do not necessarily occur under ldd, so the
process must be different.
