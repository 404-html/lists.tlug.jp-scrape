Godwin Stewart writes:

 > More to the point, you'll want to know if it responds correctly to a
 > SIGSTOP.

Unlikely, and in any case unreliable, as at such an early stage in
process initialization, the signal handlers are likely to be in
uncertain state.  It is possible, however, that the other parent
process will send the process group leader a SIGSEGV, and it's very
important that he respect that, and not attempt to access protected
regions.
