Well, these are "simple text documents", but research manuscripts,
project reports, legal contracts, etc. usually are collaborated on.
Perhaps one person writes one part and another person writes another
part and some boss makes a final pass to make it look like one person
wrote it all (though this last step is often skipped...and it shows!).

I love LaTeX and try to do as much as I can with it.  But one thing
that Microsoft Word does well that LaTeX does poorly is track changes.
 Annotating a PDF file with Adobe is not quite the same...

I'm sure Libreoffice and Openoffice can do it well, too, but I usually
send my documents "up" the chain.  If there is something that doesn't
look right, I don't want to explain to them the wonders of
Libre|Openoffice.  Upper management wants the document, not a lecture
on open source software.  Well, at least for me...  :-)
