Hello,

I few weeks ago I bought this interesting device with the idea to try
to put standard linux (=Gentoo for me) on it. After quick disassembly
and check, I luckily discovered solderpads for serial console... then
I left it in my drawer and completely forgot about it.

http://www.iodata.jp/product/pccard/readerwriter/wfs-sr01/

Today I pulled it out and quick nmap scan showed telent open. Sure
enough I logged in with admin/pass (same for http as shown in the
manual).
And guess what:

BusyBox v1.12.1 (2012-04-26 15:28:18 PHT) built-in shell (ash)

$ uname -a
Linux WFS-SR-1 2.6.21 #256 Thu Apr 25 09:35:37 CST 2013 mips unknown
$ cat /etc/versioninfo
rootfs=446
exfat/texfat=446
ioos/ioos=4471
one-ssid-pswd=446
web/web=985
uboot=5350/bootloader-mt5350-32M-2013-04-25-09-25.img
kernel=5350/kernel-rt5350-32M-2013-04-25-09-31


So, it does run linux already :-)

Now what are my chances to get IO-Data to give me the source of the firmware?
Has anyone dealt with them before?

I couldn't see any mention of GPL or Linux on their documentation, but
I found a few other products they offer CD with source code for the
cost of shipping.

Kalin.
