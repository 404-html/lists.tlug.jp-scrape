Hi,
I guess I didn't know how to use aliases and ended up having to
create a kind of mailing list to alias the info@example.com
to:
  craigoda@example.com
  joem@example.com
  kenhrd@example.com

There are several advantages to this.

interested member, sends e-mail to info@example.com and the
e-mail is sent to everyone on the list. 

Responsibility for reply is shared.  One person can reply and 
cc the list to avoid redundant work.

People aliased to info@example.com can unsubscribe themselves
without maintainer approval. 

The subject line is automatically prepended with TLUG Info Request:

Additional people can join easily.  If you try to subscribe
to the list, it will be sent to me for approval.

---

If you want to help out by volunteering to respond to questions from
interested members, simply join the list.   send to
majordomo@example.com  and include 
   subscribe information 

in the body of the message. 

The list should be used only to respond to potential member
inquiries, not for discussion.


Regards,
Craig
