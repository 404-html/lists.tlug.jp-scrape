I agree completely.  What kind of drive do you have in mind?  DAT, or
something else?  At home I use a Conner (now Seagate) SCSI drive that is
native 2 GB, up to 4 GB with compression.  It runs 25 - 30 meg per minute on
a Fireport 20 and has been completely reliable for over 2 years.  This drive
is now also available in an 8 GB version.  The external one is a bit steep,
about 75,000 yen at T-Zone, but a tape drive with enough capacity to let us
do an unattended backup on one tape is going to set us back a bit.   I'm not
sure if internal versions are available, but could check.  I would imagine
they are.   I'm not sure how this compares price-wise to DAT.   4 mm DAT
maybe wouldn't be too much more, but how much can you fit on one tape?
