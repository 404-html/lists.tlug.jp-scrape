>>>>> "kenhrd" == kenhrd  <kenhrd@example.com> writes:

    kenhrd> I had the same experience with my 3c589D PCMCIA ethernet
    kenhrd> card and Libretto 30.  The very first time I put 3c589D to
    kenhrd> Libretto's PCMCIA slot, win95 requests to install the
    kenhrd> driver.  I cancelled it - obviously win95 did not eat the
    kenhrd> needed driver - and then, no luck to install proper driver
    kenhrd> after that.  My solution was - to reinstall the whole
    kenhrd> win95 (more precisely, I copied the whole win95 system
    kenhrd> from the old HDD I replaced with the larger one).  I also
    kenhrd> wish to know the simpler solution.

I had the unfortunate pleasure of having done the same thing just
yesterday on a W95 box. I inserted the card, canceled the driver
install, and tried to install it manually, but no joy. Turns out I had
to zap the card from the registry, under:

HKEY_LOCAL_MANCHINE:Enum:PCMCIA:"card vendor"

Having done that, when I re-inserted the card, and went through the
driver install, it worked fine.
