A few weeks ago, I was commenting on Linux jdk1.1.5.  There
are now two versions, one for libc5 and one for glibc(6).

  http://www.blackdown.org/~sbb/jdk-1.1.5/

I'm downloading it now.  I got JPython working under
both debian hamm and redhat5.0.  It is pretty cool, but the
startup time of the JVM is still rather long.  At the same
time, cPython1.5 is supposed to be getting faster and faster.

--

On a different subject, I'm still looking for a reverse polish
notation X-Windows based calculator similar to an HP12C.  
I would like easy to change precision and commas after every
three digits.  It would be nice if the stack were viewable.  
I have a real nice one for the pilot, but it seems a little
much just to call up XCopilot just to use a calculator. 
There is a hardware emulator of the HP48 called x48, but it
requires the HP48 ROM to use.
