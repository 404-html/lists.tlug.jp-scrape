Q2: Do other distributions have these udev problems? I.e. is it the
kernel's fault, or Fedora's fault? Is anyone using FC6 and finding it
better than FC5 (in the "stability/things actually work" sense, not the
"cool new features" sense)?


After about one week with FC6, I have found that it sometimes fails to
detect my onboard audio (VIA 8237) (I didn't have that problem with
FC5) (after a reboot it is ok). Don't know if it is a kernel or udev
problem. Everything else seems ok so far.

Q3: Which distributions work well for multimedia, out of the box? I.e.
you can play just about any media format you find on the web, without
having to recompile things or tweak obscure configuration files, or hunt
for codecs.


It's not out of the box, but I have found it easy to become able to
play .wmv and .avi files, by downloading xine (you can get it with yum
from livna) and copying the codecs from mplayer's site. Only these two
steps, without the need to recompile anything
