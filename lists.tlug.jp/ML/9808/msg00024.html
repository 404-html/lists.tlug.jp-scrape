Ok, I have to admit that they were the cheapest my brother could
get. The contents weren`t really that important and you don`t want to
waste too much money on that. I just mentioned the whole stuff to give 
some warning what might happen with CD-Rs. The make of the CD-Rs
varied. There is no correlation between the manufacturer and the
errors. I am not really complaining much because a could watch most of 
the stuff. I was just curious and when I saw that the number of errors 
increased even after they were delivered I was a little surprised. 
Anyway, the quality was still good about the same as a video, which he 
can`t send because I don`t have a PAL vcr here (just in case you are
wondering).

If anybody is curiuos. The mpegs were made by using a Pc-card called
snazzy (http://www.dazzle.com/) which uses some mpeg encoder chips
made by DEC who told us there is no interest in Linux drivers when we
asked them for specs (they even said it is not technically possible to 
write drivers for Linux????).
