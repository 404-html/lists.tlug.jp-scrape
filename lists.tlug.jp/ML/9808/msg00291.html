Servers Get Smaller and Cheaper

Technauts earlier this week introduced a dedicated server for 
Web hosting and E-mail service that claims superfast 
performance on hardware that would, in many other contexts, 
be considered obsolete.

Technauts claims it can support 7 million hits per day on its 
eServer 150, which has a 100 MHz Pentium processor with 16 
MB of memory and 1.2 GB of storage, and which runs a 
homebrew proprietary Web and E-mail server written in C, 
on top of Linux. Available immediately, the system is priced at 
$1,895, and includes a built-in 10BaseT Ethernet connection. 

Itis designed for small businesses to set up an Internet site, and 
for departments of large businesses for Internet, extranet or 
intranet site hosting.

The unit measures eight inches square and about an inch thick. 
By Mitch Wagner
