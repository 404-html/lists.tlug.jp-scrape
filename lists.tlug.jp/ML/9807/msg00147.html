> 
> >Every time I start up Linux I'm getting a series of messages that say

> 
> I've experienced this, too.  I don't have a fix, but my workaround is to
> either use reboot -n, then wait until it gets to the POST and
> drop power (although I do not, as a rule, turn off my computers)or to
> use a keyboard reboot (which is trapped and starts the reboot -n boot
> sequence) and then drop power during the POST.
> 
> With either of these workaround, the disks are unmounted cleanly and
> bootup is normal.

you have this problem with TL i386 on a normal machine?  If you shutdown
the machine with 'shutdown -r now', 'shutdown -h now', or simply
ctrl-alt-del, the drives will unmount cleanly, as long as you don't
powercycle it before it says, "Unmounting filesystems..."
