  I'm just catching up finally on all the linux-announce digest mailings
that came in over the summer holidays, and I came across this:

	The latest release of the Linux filesystem defragmenter, defrag-0.70,
	is now available at
	linux.dcs.ed.ac.uk:/pub/linux/defrag/defrag-0.70.tar.gz
	(to appear at)
	sunsite.unc.edu:/pub/Linux/system/filesystems/defrag-0.70.tar.gz

  Has anyone used this?  I would hesistate to unleash something like this
on my actual (non-experimental) Lignux partition, which is what really
needs it (although fragmentation is still reported at under 5% - I would
imagine after all this time, it's pretty bad).
