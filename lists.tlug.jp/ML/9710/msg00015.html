> A useful thing to try is "/usr/sbin/sendmail -q -d" ... the -q tells
> sendmail that you wish to process the outgoing queue, and the -d displays
> copious amounts of debugging information to stdout (stderr?  I forget
> which).
> 
> That should pinpoint exactly why sendmail can't talk to the other machine
> ...
> 
> --      Chris (wileyc@example.com)

Thanks for your advice, Chris. I have fiddled with all sorts of things
and at last I've found out one fact. And that is so simple that I look
like a simple BAKA. When a network doesn't use DNS, such as a wee
network
at home, the sendmail should be linked to sendmail.nomx, not to
sendmail.mx.
That is that! And if necessary, sendmail.cf should be customized, but in
my case it was not necessary. Now I am all right.
