Darren Cook writes:

 > You have two 192.168.0.0 networks trying to co-exist here; that may
 > work, but if it turns out to be the problem (and you have the
 > configuration option) how about changing the wireless router to use
 > 172.16.0.0 or 10.0.0.0? I find that helps me realize the networks are
 > different.

Or use 192.168.1.0 or 192.168.111.0 -- 192.x.y.z is Class C so by
default most stacks will give it a 255.255.255.0 mask, even though the
"modern way" is CIDR (classless).

As for the problem itself, I would hazard a guess that the wireless
router doesn't know that the 4-port router is its gateway.  Try
traceroute to someplace on the internet to see what the routers think
they are doing.
