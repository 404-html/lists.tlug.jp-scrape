I find that I keep coming back to LaTeX and emacs.   I really
enjoy the flexibility.  I tried using LyX 0.10.1, but I
couldn't get the proper emacs keymappings to work.  Even have
success?  I also use MS Word under Windows sometimes, but have
never really enjoyed the experience.

One thing I wanted under LaTeX was the ability to specify a nice
Sans Serif bold font for all headers in the documentclass{article}.
I'm using the RedHat3.0.3 distribution of Linux.  I looked
in /usr/lib/texmf/texmf/tex/latex/base and copied the
article.cls and article.sty files to newarticle.cls and newarticle.sty.
I then edited the fonts that specified the headers.  To my
surprise, it worked.  :-)   I'm always amazed when this
happens.  Emboldened by the success, I searched around and
found some adobe fonts in /usr/lib/texmf/texmf/fonts/tfm/adobe

Just for fun, I played around with a palatino font.
	\begin{document}
	\newfont{\newpalatino}{pplbo8r}
	
	\newpalatino
	This is a new font
	\normalfont
	\end{document}

Hey, it worked again.  This was enough for one day, so I took a
nap.  However, I was wondering what other TLUGers did about
font specifications in their documents.  Is there some
chart that lists the fonts and matches it with a font sample?
