On Wed, May 08, 2002 at 09:47:31AM +0900, Jean-Christian Imbeault wrote:
> I've read that installing XFS on a root partition is not a simple thing 
> (not very hard bit not simple) and that it limits your rescue options if 
> your root partition ever goes bad.

Its not any harder than installing ReiserFS or JFS on a fresh RH
install. Same goes for rescue options. Its even less difficult if you
use the SGI install disks Stuart mentioned.

One procedure is to copy all the data in root on a seperate partition
and boot with 'root=' set to that partition. Then format your original /
as XFS, copy the data back and reboot as usual. Remember to modify
/etc/fstab for each step.

> Any comments from people who have XFS on their root partition?

My root is ReiserFS. Mainly because of the average file size I
mentioned earlier.
