I seem to recall a similar experience when using a serial booted Sparc.

In my case I started with NO monitor and NO kybd.   I installed Linux from
another Sparc on the network with a Mac connected to the serial port for 
the terminal.

Everything worked fine.    

Then I got a monitor and kybd for the Sparc, connected it up to the w/s and 
restarted.

The first half of the boot [upto the Appletalk part if my memory serves me 
correctly] appeared on the w/s monitor.   Then it continued on booting 
through the serial port.    So if I only had the w/s monitor on, it appeared 
as though the system had hung up - but in reality it had booted, and the 
login screen was waiting for me on the Mac [the serial port].

To get everything back to the w/s monitor and kybd I used 'setconsole'.

From what I gather you want to do the opposite.   But I couldn't tell from 
your message whether you still have the monitor connected to the IPX, or
whether you saw the 'Appletalk' msg through the serial port connection?
