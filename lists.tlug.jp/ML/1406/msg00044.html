Agree with CL.

Debian is far simpler and more reliable, yes. Plus features are more mature. 

Some suggestions in any Distro:

○ Use a VM to take updates and test first.
○ Install original disto with LVM and room for snapshots so you can roll back bad updates.
○ be practiced with your rescue media and LVM commands.
○ copy /boot with tar to /boot.tgz before LVM snap so you can unpack it after rollback of /.

On Ubuntu take distro upgrades while they're still supported.
