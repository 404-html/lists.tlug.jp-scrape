Despite the error messages saying the device doesn't exist, the real problem could be permissions. 

Dave M G wrote:


There was also a recommendation to edit a udev rules file:
dave@example.com:/dev$ more /etc/udev/rules.d/10-udev.rules #Dave Palm Pilot BUS="usb", SYSFS{serial}=="PN70UBL5V099", NAME="pilot"
Assuming you have the above entry and it is the correct one for your device, try adding a MODE setting, e.g.: 

 ... SYSFS{serial}=="PN70UBL5V099", NAME="pilot", MODE="666"

Works for me[tm].
