On Apr 3, 2006, at 10:08, David Bennett wrote:


I am wondering if anyone knows anything about Japan's caller ID
systems and if in fact their is a difference (ie. will it not work.)
I don't have access to the specs any more, but they are different. Both use FSK, but the frequencies are slightly different (Bell 202 vs V.23). The timing is also different. (The US system sends the ID between the first and second ring, and the Japanese system sends after a special short first ring and polarity reversal. I think the pause after the ring and the length of the preamble before the first data bit are also different, but that probably isn't important since nobody decodes in the analog domain any more.)
It is possible to handle both (check the zaptel work from Asterisk), but there was probably no advantage for your phone manufacturer to do so.
