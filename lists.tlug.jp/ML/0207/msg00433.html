May i say that TLUG has been serving also as a bird-of-a-feather type of
community ... as such it has unspoken traditions and its own "culture"
(it's such a cliche but i know no other way of putting it)

If you change jobs and go to a new office, then the first thing you do is
to try and find out how things are done there, right ?  If someone shows
you how to do something in one way, and you snap at him saying "well I do
it differently, don't be arrogant" then the chances are, you won't make
many friends.


> Newbies, as they may be, have every right to ask questions and seek
> help but they do not have any right to expect and answer.

If everyone understood that they may not necessarily get the kind of
answer they like, then it might help reduce the tension a little bit.

Everyone has disagreements at TLUG, it's just not "newbies".  Please don't
take technical discussions as personal attacks. In the case of "command
line" thread, honestly, if you really want to get most out of Linux, then
learning CLI is really very essential, and it is a fact, rather than an
opinion.
