One quick comment here--though INTUITIVE=what_you_are_used_to_using, I
have found FreeBSD ports to be a bit better than NetBSD's, and found
that I can imitate almost all of Gentoo's portage things (that I use)
with it.  FreeBSD's ports seem a bit more flexible.  (I could easily be
wrong on this though).  Just as an example, make extract in NetBSD
doesn't seem to stop with simply unpacking the port, while it does in
FreeBSD.  

Not being a programmer however, I could easily be misinterpreting what I
see when I do a make extract. 

Also, and I am NOT Gentoo bashing--I found during a recent install that
a lot of things that were broken have been fixed--that FreeBSD's ports
tend to have fewer problems than Gentoo's.  That is, even in CURRENT
almost all ports install without me having to google to find out what
went wrong.
