On Tue, 10 Nov 1998, Atlas21 Shawn wrote:

>I've been following this thread with some interest, since I have an NEC TA
>and will be installing Red Hat this evening.

I just got some NEC TA init strings here (try them in this order, use the
first one that works).

1) ATQ0V1X3$N1=1
2) AT$N1=1
3) AT$N1=1$N11=0


>In addition to the actual connection problem, I was wondering if anyone
>here is successfully using fax functions with their ISDN.
>I have been running Win95, but since I got ISDN I have not been able to get
>fax functionality, and friends that I have asked about
>it say they they too have been unsuccessfuly in getting it to work in the
>Win95/ISDN environment.

I used to do it all the time and it works just fine, but since Windows
tech questions are totally off-topic, I have no further comment on making it
work under Windows :-). I see no reason why it shouldn't work under Linux,
though, since that is just ordinary fax dialing and ISDN doesn't really enter
the picture (that is, the fax software neither knows nor cares what's on the
other side of the fax modem).  So just fire up your favorite Linux fax
software and have at it.
