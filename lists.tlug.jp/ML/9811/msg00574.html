On Fri, 20 Nov 1998, Michael Casinghino wrote:

> I have moral objections to making distinctions between /usr/local/ and
> /usr/.  I mean isn't _everything_ local?  Really?  Is there a reason I
> shouldn't do this?
> 

well, the distinction is a historical one.  sort of.  basically, /usr is
for stuff that's part of the core OS, ie, when you install TurboLinux, it
doesn't put any files in /usr/local, since a distribution shouldnt do
that.  /usr/local is for stuff that you go out and download and install
yourself - ie, third party stuff that's not packaged by us (or any other
distribution vendor).
