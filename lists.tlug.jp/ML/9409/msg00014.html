New:

How're peoples window managers going?  I recently switched over to
fvwm and I'm enjoying it quite a lot, I've modified many of the menus and
key mappings, so now with the quick click of a key or two I can swap
between virtual desktops, or even scroll around the XFREE virtual screen.
I can also bring windows to front or back with a key press, very convienient
with my limited 640X480 thinkpad screen.


Anyone start playing with TeX yet?  I have, it's a pain in the butt,
but I'm starting to get used to it.  I think I'll buy the TeX Book and the
LaTeX manual when I have a chance.  I'm written a 15 page document in TeX
with symbols, bulleted lists, sections, subsections, etc, but it took a
long tome to get used to and I had to write a lot of macros to do what I
wanted.  Much of this is available in LaTex, but since the only documentation
I had was the Gentle Guide to TeX I wanted to write it in TeX only, not LaTeX.
Anyway, it's possible, but if you want to do it regularly, definitely buy
the real books.  The Gentle guide it nice, but just scrapes the surface and
left me with more questions than answers.  I ended up learning a lot more
by looking at the TeX code itself than the formatted output.  BTW, a
wondergul tool is xdvi with allows to you view dvi files directly without
printing or converting to PostScript first.  It was installed on my machine
with the TeX package.
