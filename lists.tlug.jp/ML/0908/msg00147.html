But another, very important purpose of publishing is to communicate
the idea to those who will actually produce an innovation available to
society.  You are entirely neglecting that fact, which is enshrined in

Like the subprimes "innovation". If that's good for the US, that's good for the rest of the world too.
And, basically, innovations are not "available to society". They are available to those who can afford them. And businesses are very careful to select who can afford their innovations and on what terms. Look at Monsanto. Like Big Pharma.
Business in general totally fails to make innovations available to _society_ as a whole. It's only purpose is to make money, availability to society is a side effect (and sometimes a very nasty one). And you see what kind of innovations make the most money right now: the one that cannot be further removed from society: financial tricks that put tons of moneys in the pockets of the trickers. And this money certainly does not come from the taxes they pay...
