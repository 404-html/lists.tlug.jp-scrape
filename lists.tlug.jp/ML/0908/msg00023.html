> I am also one of those people that is not very impressed with Japanese
> banks in general. These questions used to regularly go through my head
> all of the time when I was using UFJ, SMBC, 東京都民銀行, and Mizuho:
> 
> * Why do the ATMs close at midnight or over holidays? Are there little
> people in there operating them manually that need to close up and
> catch the last train home? I wouldn't be surprised.

I believe the official reason is because they couldn't guarantee
customer security out of office hours.  This was the reason given to why
ATM's in convenience stores could offer 24 hour ATM service.

> * Why I am charged for using my own bank's ATM to withdrawal money
> later in the evening or over the weekend?

Because they can.  In the last 10 years Japanese banks have improved
massively.  If you go with an internet bank like SBI you have 24 hour
access and free withdrawal/deposit from 7-11 ATMs and pretty good
internet banking.  The catch being that all information is in Japanese
and there are no branches you can go to.

> * Why I am charged for making a transfer to someone using the same bank?

I don't believe this is the case with SBI but their might be a limit on
the number of transfers.

> * Not too annoyed by this one, but why do they all close their doors at 3PM?

As above, because they can.  The conspiracy theorists (realists;) have
it that they were all colluding to keep banking service cost high and
services poor.  I think the real reason had more to do with it being a
non-profit making business they were forced to support.  When postal
savings came in,  it was outside this, and offered slightly better
services and lower service costs to the chagrin of the banks.  Since
then a number of low cost banks have sprung up.

> Alexei just mentioned this a post or so back but I just recently got
> an account with Shinsei and was extremely impressed for all the
> reasons already mentioned plus a few more.

Shinsei is OK, when they first came out they were the only bank offering
this sort of low cost banking service.  Shinsei concerns me because, I
presume they are running the banking services at a loss and the
investment products[1], that I presume is their intended revenue stream,
weren't very competitive.
