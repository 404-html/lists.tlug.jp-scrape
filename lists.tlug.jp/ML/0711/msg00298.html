Drew Hamilton wrote:

I hadn't twigged to the "no backlight" problem before I read this one ... 

It's not supposed to have a backlight.  The point of the e-ink display
is that it's supposed to very closely mimic the look of paper, and
regular books have no backlight.  I think this is probably the reason
that I can read books on an e-ink display for hours without my eyes
getting tired.

In that respect, perhaps it has mimicked a real book just a little too well. It means that you need a good outside light source to make it readable. Which is, I suppose good news for those companies that make the little clip on LED lamps for reading in bed without waking your SO ... and battery makers.
