Godwin,

That's because it's not in a regular user's $PATH, only in root's path.
As I said earlier, you have to ***BE*** root, not just have root's
permissions.

So, either run "su -" and enter your *root* password (not your regular
user's password) and run the command, or log out, then log back in as
root and do the necessary.
