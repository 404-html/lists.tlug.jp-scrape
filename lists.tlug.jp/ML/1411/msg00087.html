Apparently, the correct answer to solving the problem is "Send a message to TLUG."
Short story long, before I could try any of the above, I re-checked the settings of every installed add-on I have to see if there were any possible conflicts that I'd missed before. Didn't see anything obviously in conflict.
I then re-checked my super compact theme* settings and found that the programmer who'd designed it had posted a Mk. II version two days ago. He hadn't upgraded the existing design because he'd built a new coding template that he thinks will automatically follow changes that Mozilla adds on the fly. So, I installed the new version and all of my playback problems went away -- on Cnet, Stratfor, Gizmag, ThinkGeeks ... and the two dozen, or so, other sites I tried before writing this mea culpa.
Anyway, I tried the Good Doctor's command line start and got no relevant error messages. At all. The messages I _did_ get had nothing to do with any of this and were actually things I know how to fix, and did. 

So, I decided to pop the top on a Heineken and call it fixed.

To answer Travis, in it's last incarnation, I was seeing black rectangles where the video should have been. When I permitted content from video providers (I use extremely strict initial NoScript settings and permit only what is absolutely needed on a page-by-page basis) I would occasionally see the turning arrow or the circle of dots that indicated content loading but then the box would go black again and all activity would stop. Prior to that, there were a couple of other incarnations in which the progress bar would appear across the bottom a'la You Tube, and sound would play but no picture was seen. This morphed into the first condition described above.
