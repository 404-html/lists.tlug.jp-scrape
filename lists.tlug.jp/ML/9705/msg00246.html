On Sun, May 25, 1997 at 11:22:08PM +0900, Dennis McMurchy wrote:
>   This is kind of an unimportant little detail, but it bothers me.
> In the headers of all my outgoing mail is the line:
> 
> X-Authentication-Warning: tangrending: denismcm owned process doing -bs
> 
>  tangrending (=tojinmachi) is the hostname here, and denismcm is my
> usual login.  It seems really unnecessary for this line to go out with

To treat the symptom, the easiest way is to make denismcm a "trusted" user.
If you are hacking an existing /etc/sendmail.cf, look for a "Trusted users"
section.  It will include either something like Ft/etc/sendmail.ct (which
means you should add denismcm to the specified file) or a series of entries
like:
Troot
Tdaemon
Tuucp
add
Tdenismcm

If you are making your own sendmail.cf, add
FEATURE(use_ct_file)
