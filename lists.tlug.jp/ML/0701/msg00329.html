On Tue, 23 Jan 2007, Dave M G wrote:

First, Windows codecs for most video files, especially AVI: 

Just as a note: AVI is a container, not a codec. The video content
within the AVI container could be encoded with any of a number of
codecs. The same goes for the audio.

This means, for example, that you may not be able to play one AVI file,
but might be able to play another. This tends to be quite confusing to
people until things are explained.

BTW, I'm very pleased to hear about your success with getting a

a similar conversion experiment with office software, I'm sure we'd all
