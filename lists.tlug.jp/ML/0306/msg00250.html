On Thursday, June 26, 2003, at 10:59  AM, Jean-Christian Imbeault wrote:

> I want to build a Linux router/firewall box for a 100 Mb line we have 
> at the office. I was thinking of setting up a minimal RH install, 
> setting up routing tables and iptables.
>
> I found the how-to and am reading through it but I also found 
> something called FREESCO (Free Cisco).
>
> Does anyone have any experience with FREESCO. Would you recommend it?
>

Freesco is great. I used it when I had ISDN, but had to switch when I 
got DSL. (I don't think it will do PPPoE or ADSL. It didn't at that 
time.)
(Also, IIRC, I read it would only use your NIC at 10Mb, but I'm not 
sure about that now.)
Take a look at IPCOP (www.ipcop.org) if Freesco won't do what you need.
I switched over to IPCop and haven't had a problem at all.  Very simple 
setup and administration.
