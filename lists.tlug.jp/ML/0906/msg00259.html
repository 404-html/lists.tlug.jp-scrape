TLUG,

I'm sure at least some of you are aware of the service "StumbleUpon":

http://www.stumbleupon.com/
Recently, I've been finding more ways to up my Japanese, and I thought that being able to use StumbleUpon in Japanese would be fantastic for entertaining myself while practising.
However, I've looked into it, and as far as I can tell, there is no way to set StumbleUpon to be in Japanese. Sometimes you get random Japanese-related-content (Usually *about* Japan, not *in* Japanese) mixed in with the rest of it, but no way to focus on Japanese language content specifically. 

Also, there does not seem to be a Japanese StumbleUpon equivalent.
Am I wrong about either of those? It seems kind of hard to believe that there really isn't a Japanese StumbleUpon of any sort.
I'm an SU addict and I believe you're right. The only language section I've seen is labeled "Languages I speak" in the profile page but it looks like that's just to display on your profile and has no effect on the language of stumbled pages.
The closest thing I can think of is Hatena bookmarks http://b.hatena.ne.jp but it's more like the old Digg (without the random button) than SU.
