On 13/08/07, Kim Ahlström <kim.ahlstrom@example.com> wrote:

> 2) When writing the HTML I used DoCoMo's i-mode guide[2] as a starting
> point.

That is fine, but just be aware that the i-mode browsers tend to
support a little more than the others. The main idea is to keep it
simple, stupid and avoid tables. There are little tricks like
de-XHTMLing tags, e.g. <br /> => <br>, etc.; but by and large, if you
use simple HTML, you will be fine.

> Is there an easy way to do this?

No. But there is a way to do it, provided you can spoof an HTTP
request. Hint: visit jisho.org with your AU phone and dump all of the
HTTP headers that it sent. Take the useful stuff from there and try
injecting those headers into your request from your PC browser. Seems
that Firefox has an extension to allow this.

Your web browser must send a unique ID in the HTTP headers (every
Japanese keitai does this, since DoCoMo does not support cookies).
