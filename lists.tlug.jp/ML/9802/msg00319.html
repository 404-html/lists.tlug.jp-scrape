I agree Craig's approach is the most practical.  

Let's think which TLUG domain we will prefer to go after that.
"ORG" domain does not imply the "Organization" is in US.  
It is simply a Top Level Domain (TLD).

Like I did for my own domain (Hayama Net), the setting at InterNIC
is just within one day - 12 hours in my case.  The registration for
two years is just $100.00 since we already have PHT who kindly
hosts us, and ns.inovis.de for secondary DNS.  We will be charged
$50 every year after the third year.  No need of documentation like
consitution, just application over e-mail will be accepted.
"ORG" can be quick and less expensive.

In case of "JP", JPNIC fees are :- 
(http://www.nic.ad.jp/jpnic/faq/QandA.html#4010)
     Domain registration: 20,000 Yen 
There seems no annual fee in case of JPNIC.

As Smack Omote mentioned, the new domain space "GR.JP" (GR stands
for group) is there.  Just application and seal certificate (Inkan
shomei) of the Daihyousha (President) and FukuDaihyousha (vice
President) are required.  No need to go through contitution or the
like.  I think non-Japanese guys can have their signatures certified
by the consulate.

So, if we want "JP", accept "GR" (group) instead of "OR" 
(organization), and can afford 20,000 Yen, why don't we go for
TLUG.GR.JP.
