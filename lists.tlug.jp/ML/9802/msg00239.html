This looks great, a transparency projector will work fine.  I can print
out slides before I leave. (Since they're just HTML pages, I'll also
post them and mail out the url.)

I think the talk will be something along the lines of evangelism -
encouranging non-English speakers to get involved and organized,
processe & resources - how the whole source submission thing is going to
work and what resources are available, and planning - where is
communicator week in language support and what the i18n group here
thinks is the direction the source base ought to move in.

My biggest goal is to evangelize the whole free source thing, and
particularly get non-US developers involved and active participants.  I
would hope that we could work together to start a core of i18n-type
developers who are large enough to have lobbying power with the mainters
of the source base, without this I have a big concern that the browser
is going to take a big step backwards as far as i18n support.  In the
US, there are already groups organizing themselves around platforms and
areas of functionality.  I think we should try to get non-US developers
mobilized around supporting and developing functionality to make the
browser work well with languages other than english.

I would hope that anyone who is interested - even if they don't program
- would show up.

/t
