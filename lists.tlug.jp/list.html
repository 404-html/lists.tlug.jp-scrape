TLUG is a community built by people who respect each other and enjoy using Linux. These mailing lists are places where people learn more about Linux in a friendly, cooperative manner. By joining our mailing list, you agree to abide by the TLUG mailing list policy, so please read it carefully. We're looking forward to meeting you!

TLUG

[ list info ]

Main technical mailing list. Serves as a forum for Linux, *BSD (FreeBSD, NetBSD and OpenBSD), Solaris, Unix, and general discussions about programming, Open Source, and Stuff That Matters to geeks (apologies to Slashdot).

Subscribe by sending an email to <tlug-request@tlug.jp> with a Subject line of "subscribe" (without the quotes), or by visiting the list info page and following the directions in the Subscribing to Tlug section. (Unsubscribe by emailing with the subject line "unsubscribe" (without the quotes) or by visiting the bottom of the list info page.)

Lingo

[ list info ]

Discussion of issues related to the languages (natural languages; for programming languages, see the main TLUG list) of which the group as a whole has extensive knowledge and to cultural and administrative dealings in the countries concerned.

Subscribe by sending an email to <lingo-request@tlug.jp> with a Subject line of "subscribe" (without the quotes), or by visiting the list info page and following the directions in the Subscribing to Lingo section.

TLUG-admin

[ list info ]

Discussion of administrative issues. Anyone is welcome to join; we would love your help reviewing announcements, proposing public activities (including technical meeting speakers), and finding out how/where/when to help with maintenance work on the server or website.

Subscribe by sending an email to <tlug-admin-request@tlug.jp> with a Subject line of "subscribe" (without the quotes), or by visiting the list info page and following the directions in the Subscribing to tlug-admin section.
